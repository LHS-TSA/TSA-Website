About TSA

What is TSA?

TSA stands for Technoloy Student Association and is a club devoted to teaching students to lead in a technical world. The orginzation engages students in science, technoly, engineering, and math (STEM) activities like: robotics, structural desing, transportation modeling, systems control, and many more. There are currently more than 250,000 members enrolled in 2,000 middle and high schools across 48 states and even chapters in Germany, Turkey, and Japan.

TSA's History

The Technology Student Association (TSA) began in 1958 as the American Industrial Arts Student Association (AIASA), a student organization dedicated to students involved in technology education from kindergarten through high school. For the next twenty years, the American Industrial Arts Association sponsored activities of AIASA. In 1978, AIASA, Inc., a nonprofit organization formed to oversee AIASA activities. For the next decade, AIASA grew in size, strength, structure, and impact on students and secondary school programs. In the Summer of 1988, in its third decade, the AIASA changed the name of the organization to the Technology Student Association (TSA), the name by which it is known today. Since then, TSA has become the oldest and one of the largest student organizations in the country.

The TSA Emblem

The TSA eblem was created by GA TSA director Steve Price and adopted by the national confrence in 1989 the tsa emblem represents:

  • Scarlet (red) represents the strength and determination of the Technology Education in students and teachers to obtain their goal.

  • White represents high standards, morals, and religious beliefs.

  • Navy (blue) represents the sincerity of the Technology Education in students and teachers to obtain a greater knowledge of our technological world.

To read more about the emblem visit the TSA Bylaws and find Article XI Section 1 and 2
